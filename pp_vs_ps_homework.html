📌 Grammar Reference

Present Perfect

  • have / has + past participle
  • Experience: I have been to Rome.
  • Recent result: She has lost her keys.
  • Unfinished time: He has lived here for 3 years.
  • Time words: ever, never, just, already, yet, for, since, recently, so far, this week / year

Past Simple

  • verb + -ed or irregular form
  • Finished action at a specific time: I went to Rome in 2019.
  • Series of past events: He arrived, sat down, and opened his laptop.
  • Time words: yesterday, last night / week / year, in 2020, ago, when I was…, in the morning

⚡ Key contrast

PP  "I have visited Paris." → experience, no specific time
PS  "I visited Paris in 2022." → specific finished time
PP  "She has just called you." → recent, result still relevant
PS  "She called you yesterday." → finished, specific time
