Exercise 1
Fill in the blank — -ed or -ing?

Complete each sentence with the correct form of the adjective in brackets.

🔑 Remember

-ing → describes the thing / situation that causes the feeling.

-ed → describes how a person feels.

1. The end of Inside Out 2 is so (move) — I actually cried!
2. She felt really (embarrass) when she sent 😍 to her teacher.
3. This documentary about emojis is genuinely (fascinate) — I watched it twice.
4. I was really (surprise) to learn that 👍 can be rude in some countries.
5. It can be very (confuse) when your friend uses 🙂 but isn't actually happy.

Exercise 3
Correct the mistake ✍️

Each sentence has one wrong adjective (underlined). Rewrite the full sentence correctly in the box below it.

1. "I was so boring during the three-hour lecture that I nearly fell asleep."
✅ Model answer: "I was so bored during the three-hour lecture that I nearly fell asleep." (You feel bored → use -ed)
2. "The cultural differences between countries are quite surprised when you first learn about them."
✅ Model answer: "The cultural differences between countries are quite surprising when you first learn about them." (The situation causes surprise → use -ing)
3. "My friend was very embarrassing when she realised she'd sent 💀 to her grandma."
✅ Model answer: "My friend was very embarrassed when she realised she'd sent 💀 to her grandma." (She felt embarrassed → use -ed)
