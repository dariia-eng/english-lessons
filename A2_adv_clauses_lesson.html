Scene 2 · Grammar
✨ Adverbial Clauses
Give your sentences more detail — and more power.
What are they?
An adverbial clause gives you more information about the main clause in a sentence.
A conjunction connects the adverbial clause to the main clause.

"Eddy doesn't like rugby because he thinks it's too rough."
   ↳ main clause  +  conjunction  +  adverbial clause
📌 Key rule: The conjunction can go in the middle or at the start of the sentence. When it goes at the start, add a comma after the adverbial clause.
"Although Leo was hungry, he hardly touched his food."
Three types of adverbial clause
Type Conjunctions Example
When things happen
when as just as before after
You called me just as I was thinking about you.
Why things happen
because so that
We should text each other so that we can plan when to meet.
A contrast
although
Although Leo was hungry, he hardly touched his food.

🌟 You've Got This!
Let's review what you've learned today.
Today's Key Language
Adverbial clauses give more information about the main clause.
A conjunction connects them.

When things happen:  when · as · just as · before · after
Why things happen:  because · so that
A contrast:  although
📌 Remember: You can put the conjunction at the START or in the MIDDLE.
→ "He hurt himself as he was cycling to school."
→ "As he was cycling to school, he hurt himself."
