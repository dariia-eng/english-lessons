Speaking — Ex 4

Dear Agony Aunt…

Someone has written in for advice. Your job: write the reply — using conflict vocabulary.

📬 Advice column  |  Letter received this week
Dear Agony Aunt,

I need your help. My best friend contradicts everything I say. If I like a film, she hates it. If I suggest a restaurant, she picks a different one. Last week she picked a fight with me in front of our whole friend group over something completely trivial — the music at a party.

I've tried to talk to her about it, but she either backs down immediately and then does the same thing again, or she gets defensive and accuses me of being unreasonable. Our friends have started ganging up on me, saying I'm "too sensitive." I don't think the underlying issue is the music or the films. I think she just can't stand it when I'm happy.

We've been friends for ten years. I don't want to clash with her — but I can't come to terms with this behaviour any more. What should I do?
— Exhausted in Edinburgh

💬 Language for your reply

It sounds like the underlying issue is… Have you tried to mediate by…? You may need to pick your moment… Rather than backing down again, try… A compromise might be to… Sometimes you have to come to terms with…

✍️ Your reply — discuss with a partner:

1. What's the real underlying issue? 2. Should she clash directly or find another way? 3. Is a compromise even possible here? 4. What would YOU do in her position?

Speaking — Ex 9

Advice Clinic

Someone's in a conflict and needs your help. What do you tell them?

🩺 Your teacher reads each situation. Give your honest advice — what should the person do or say? Be specific. Use vocabulary from the lesson.
Your flatmate plays music loudly until 2am. You mentioned it once and they laughed it off. You feel completely disrespected — but you don't want to seem unreasonable. What do you do?
A colleague keeps taking credit for your ideas in meetings. You find it outrageous, but they're also a friend. You've said nothing so far. What would you say — and when?
You've been in a pointless argument with a family member for three weeks. Neither of you will back down. It's getting ridiculous. What's your advice?
A close friend is always 40–50 minutes late. You've never said anything because you don't want to seem unreasonable. But you're close to walking away from the friendship. What should happen next?

💬 Useful language

If I were you, I'd… The most sensible thing would be to… I think the underlying issue is… It's not unreasonable to feel… At some point you need to back down and… They need to come to terms with the fact that…
