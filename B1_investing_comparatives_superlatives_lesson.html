Reading

Bitcoin: Brilliant Investment or Big Mistake?

Read the article. Pay attention to the words in bold.

Should You Put Your Money in Crypto?
Personal Finance Weekly

Ten years ago, one Bitcoin was worth less than $100. Today, it is worth tens of thousands of dollars. For the people who bought early, it was the most profitable investment in history. Stories like this make crypto very attractive — and that is exactly the problem.

Bitcoin is also the most volatile major asset in the world. In 2022, it lost more than 65% of its value in twelve months. That is a worse drop than almost any stock market crash in the last fifty years. If you invested €10,000 at the top, you had less than €3,500 twelve months later. Unlike stocks, crypto has no company behind it, no earnings, and no yield. Its price moves on news, social media, and emotion — nothing more.

Supporters say this is too simple. They argue that Bitcoin is "digital gold" — a better store of value than traditional currencies because no government can print more of it. They also say that a small amount of crypto — around 5–10% of a portfolio — gives more diversification without too much extra risk. And with higher liquidity than property, it is easier to buy and sell than almost any other asset.

The question is not really whether crypto is good or bad. It is whether you can sleep at night knowing your money can lose half its value in a month. If yes — it might deserve a small place in your portfolio. If not, safer investments exist. And remember: the most dangerous investor is the one who buys because of fear of missing out.

Grammar Focus

Comparative & Superlative

We use comparatives to compare two things. We use superlatives to compare one thing with all others in a group.

Comparative — comparing two things

adj + -er + than  /  more + adj + than
➤ Crypto is riskier than government bonds.
➤ Bitcoin is more volatile than gold.

Superlative — comparing one thing with all others

the + adj + -est  /  the most + adj
➤ FOMO is the most dangerous reason to invest.
➤ Bitcoin was the best performing asset of the last decade.
AdjectiveTypeComparativeSuperlative
safeshort (1 syllable)saferthe safest
