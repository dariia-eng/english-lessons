A Very Bad Evening

Read this online review. Underline any phrases that describe what happened TO the writer (not what they actively did).
⭐ 1/5 — CineWorld Cinema, Review posted online

Last Friday, my partner and I decided to see Gladiator II for a special anniversary dinner-and-film night. What followed was, without question, the worst cinema experience I have ever had.

To begin with, our pre-booked seats had been given to someone else. We were told by a member of staff that "the system had been updated" and that our booking had been cancelled — something we were never informed about. No apology was offered.

Eventually, we were moved to seats in the very front row — the only ones left. During the film, the sound system broke down twice. No explanation was given for the interruptions, and no refunds were issued. When I approached the front desk, I was kept waiting for over fifteen minutes while the staff member dealt with a phone call.

I was eventually handed a complaints form — a piece of paper — and told to "fill it in and post it." In 2025. A piece of paper.

Several complaints have apparently been submitted about this venue already. I have since found out that the cinema was awarded "Best Local Cinema" last year. I find this hard to believe. I will not be returning.

Comprehension — discuss

  • What exactly went wrong? List at least four specific problems.
  • What compensation, if any, was the writer offered?
  • Which complaint do you think was the most serious? Why?
  • How would you have reacted in this situation?

Passive Voice

📐 The Passive Voice — Complete the Table

Subject + to be (tense) + past participle (+ by + agent)

Fill in the passive form for each tense. Type your answer and press Tab or click away to check.

Tense Active Passive — type here
Present simple They cancel bookings. Bookings .
Past simple Staff gave our seats away. Our seats .
Present perfect They have ignored my complaint. My complaint .
Past perfect They had cancelled the booking. The booking .
Future (will) They will issue a refund. A refund .
Modal verbs They should address this. This .
Why use the passive in complaints?
① The doer is unknown: "My bag was stolen."
② The doer is obvious: "The order was delivered late."
③ We want to focus on the problem: "I was kept waiting." (more impactful)
④ It sounds more formal / objective in written complaints.

Behind the Scenes

Take your role card and role-play the conversation. Use passive voice where it feels natural.

Work in pairs. Choose a scenario. Student A takes the customer/employee card; Student B takes the manager/director card. Swap after 3–4 minutes.

🎭 Scenario 1 — The Cinema

Student A — Customer
You booked tickets for Dune: Part Two online. When you arrived:
  • You were not sent a confirmation email
  • Your seats were given to someone else
  • You were made to wait 25 mins
  • You were offered popcorn as compensation — not a refund

You want: a full refund + written apology.

Student B — Cinema Manager
You are the duty manager. You know:
  • The booking system was updated last night
  • Several bookings were affected
  • Refunds can only be processed online
  • You have been given no authority to give cash refunds

Try to calm the situation. Offer what you can.

🎭 Scenario 2 — The Film Set

Student A — Junior Actor
You're on set (imagine Alien: Romulus). Problems:
  • You weren't given your script until this morning
  • Your costume was damaged in storage
  • You were told the call time was 6am, but shooting started at 5am
  • Your concerns have been ignored twice already

You want proper communication and a new costume.

Student B — Production Director
You're under pressure — the film is behind schedule:
  • Scripts were sent by email — you're not sure if they were received
  • The costume department has been overwhelmed
  • The schedule was changed late last night
  • Everyone has been affected, not just this actor

Apologise, but keep things moving.

Language to use

I wasn't informed that…making your point
No explanation was given for…highlighting the problem
I was told… / I was promised…referring to expectations
This should have been dealt with…expressing disappointment
I'm afraid this isn't acceptable.formal pushback
Could you confirm that…will be resolved?requesting action

🎬 Lesson Summary

Effective complaint = right language + right tone + right grammar

The passive voice distances the speaker from blame, focuses attention on the problem, and creates a more formal, objective tone — perfect for written and formal spoken complaints.

Take-away expressions:
"I was not informed…"  /  "No explanation was given…"  /  "This should have been dealt with…"  /  "I have been waiting for…"  /  "A refund has not been issued…"
