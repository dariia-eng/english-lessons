Warm-up — How was your week?

~10 min · Review

🔁 Quick grammar recall — comparatives

from last lesson
Small difference
slightly / a bit + comparative

"This week was slightly more stressful than last week."

Big difference
much / far / by far + comparative

"Monday was by far the worst day."

Similarity / difference
as + adj + as  ·  not as + adj + as

"I was not as tired as I expected to be."

Double comparative
The + comparative, the + comparative

"The busier I got, the more productive I felt."

Also remember:  less of a + noun  → "It was less of a problem than I thought."

Read the texts. What caused each disagreement?

Reading
When was the last time you had a minor disagreement with someone you know, or a stranger? The chances are it was not that long ago. But was it really worth it? Surely life is too short to worry about the small things! We asked three readers to tell us about their experiences.
1

I was already a vegetarian when I first met my future mother-in-law and straightaway we didn't see eye to eye. She told me she thought vegetarianism was childish and ridiculous. I was brought up not to contradict my elders, but I really had an issue with what she said. I told her vegetarianism was sensible and ethical and that she should try it. Instead, she tried to tempt me with all sorts of tasty meat dishes from her part of the country. I refused to eat any of them.

2

A couple of years ago, I clashed with my co-workers. It was a tricky situation because I was only an intern, but they gave me loads of photocopying to do from the start. After a week I told them I wasn't going to do any more because I wasn't learning anything. This caused a big argument of course, and some of my co-workers ganged up on me and said I was being disrespectful. But in the end my boss intervened and they backed down. Strangely I got a wonderful card from them when I left.

3

My flatmate picks a fight with me every time I cook because she says I make a big mess. Actually, I do clear up but not always immediately. Usually I want to enjoy my meal first and relax. Why should I be apologetic about that? It's not exactly the crime of the century! We should probably try to find a compromise, but that's difficult because the underlying issue is that she's a hopeless control freak and I'm not.

Speaking

For each scenario, tell your teacher what you would do. Use at least two phrases from the lesson in your answer. Click a card to open it.

🍽️ Scenario 1 — Family dinner

▼

You're at a family dinner. Your aunt makes a comment about your lifestyle that you completely disagree with. Your parents give you a look that says don't say anything. What do you do?

Do you contradict her? Back down? Have you been in a similar situation?

Phrases to use:
contradicthad an issue withbacked downdidn't see eye to eye

🏠 Scenario 2 — Flatmates

▼

You share a flat with two friends. One never does any cleaning. When you bring it up, the other flatmate sides with them and says you're overreacting. How do you handle it?

Is there an underlying issue? Can you find a compromise? Who should intervene?

Phrases to use:
ganged up onunderlying issuefind a compromiseclashed with

🎓 Scenario 3 — Seminar

▼

You're in a seminar. Another student confidently states something you know is factually wrong. The teacher doesn't correct them. Do you say something?

What if the student picks a fight with you afterwards? Do you intervene or let it go?

Phrases to use:
contradictpicks a fightintervenedhad an issue with

🤝 Scenario 4 — Old argument

▼

You and a friend have been arguing for weeks about something small. Neither of you will give in. How does it end?

Is one person always the first to back down in a friendship? Is that fair?

Phrases to use:
