🎥 Behind the Camera
Passive Voice — do you remember it?
Quick Reminder
We use the passive voice when we focus on what happened, not who did it.

Present simple:  is / are + past participle
   ↳ "The film is directed by a famous director."

Past simple:  was / were + past participle
   ↳ "The film was made in 2024."
💡 Tip: Past participles of regular verbs = verb + -ed  (direct → directed, release → released).
Irregular ones: make → made, write → written, see → seen, sell → sold

Scene 3 · Grammar
✨ Meet Reflexive Pronouns!
When the subject and object are the same person.
What are Reflexive Pronouns?
We use reflexive pronouns when the subject and the object of a sentence refer to the same person or thing.

"Barbie looks at herself in the mirror."  → Barbie looks at Barbie ✓
Subject Pronoun Reflexive Pronoun Example from film world
I myself "I told myself not to cry at Inside Out 2... I failed."
you yourself "Did you enjoy yourself at the cinema?"
he himself "Wonka taught himself how to make chocolate."
she herself "Moana proved to herself that she was brave."
it itself "The story explains itself through emotions."
we ourselves "We bought ourselves popcorn before the film."
you (plural) yourselves "Did you all enjoy yourselves?"
they themselves "The characters express themselves through music."
Two Main Uses
Use 1 — Subject = Object (most common)
When someone does something to themselves:
↳ "Elphaba hurt herself trying to cast the spell."
↳ "The fans found themselves crying at the end."
Use 2 — Emphasis (optional)
To say that the subject did something personally — no one else:
↳ "I myself have seen Wicked three times!"
↳ "She designed her costume herself."
Bonus — "by + reflexive" = alone / without help
↳ "Moana sailed across the ocean by herself."
↳ "Did you make this costume by yourself?"
⚠️ Common mistakes to avoid:
✗ "I enjoyed me."  →  ✓ "I enjoyed myself."
✗ "She looked at her in the mirror."  →  ✓ "She looked at herself." (same person!)
✗ "They made themself popcorn."  →  ✓ "They made themselves popcorn."

Scene 7 · Speaking
🌟 Your Time to Shine!
Use reflexive pronouns naturally in conversation.
Activity A — Discussion Questions

Talk to your partner. Try to use reflexive pronouns in your answers! The sentence starters can help you.

🎬Think about your favourite film or a film you saw recently. Did the main character do something by themselves that impressed you?
In [film], [character] did ... by herself / himself / themselves... I think it was brave because...
✨Have you ever done something completely by yourself that made you feel proud? What was it?
Once, I did ... by myself... I felt really proud of myself because...
🍿If you could direct a film yourself, what would it be about? Who would play the main character?
If I directed a film myself, it would be about... The main character would find herself / himself...
💭Which film character do you think is the best at expressing themselves? Why?
I think [character] expresses herself / himself by... She / He is different because...
Activity B — Role Play 🎭

Work with a partner. Student A is a journalist. Student B just saw their favourite film. Act out the interview! Use reflexive pronouns where possible.

🎙 Student A — Journalist
Ask your partner:
"Did you enjoy yourself at the cinema?"
"Did you buy yourself anything?"
"Did the character do something by herself/himself?"
"Can you describe the film itself in 3 words?"
🌟 Student B — Film Fan
Answer the journalist's questions.
Try to use:
"I enjoyed myself so much because..."
"I bought myself ..."
"The character herself / himself ..."
"I cried by myself in the dark!"
🌟 Remember: The goal is to speak naturally. It's OK to make mistakes — that's how you learn! Try to use at least 3 reflexive pronouns in your conversation.
